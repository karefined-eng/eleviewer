Welcome to EleViewer: The Sovereignty Workstation 🐘

> "Knowledge is only powerful when it is accessible, structured, and entirely under your control."

Welcome to EleViewer, a minimalist, blazing-fast study workspace designed to get out of your way and let your mind work. It was built with a core philosophy: Data Sovereignty. Your notes, your PDFs, your data—they all live locally on your machine. Every file your professor throws at you. One free app. Everything a student needs to read, write, and organize coursework — and nothing that slows an old laptop down. No telemetry, no forced cloud accounts, and no loading screens.

The Story Behind the Build

EleViewer wasn't built in a corporate boardroom. It was forged in the fire of academic necessity at the University of Ghana. We realized that modern productivity tools were bloated, slow, and constantly trying to lock us into their cloud ecosystems.

We needed a tool that was:
Lightweight: Fast enough to run on any student laptop.
Unified: Capable of reading PDFs, editing Markdown, and crunching CSVs in one clean interface.
Private: No mandatory telemetry, no forced cloud syncs.

Thus, the Sovereignty Workstation was born.

⚡ Master the "REFLEX" Keys

To truly unlock EleViewer's speed, you must master the core REFLEX shortcuts. Keep your hands on the keyboard and let the workstation flow with your thoughts:

Ctrl + Q : The Quick Switcher
Never reach for the mouse to find a file. Press Ctrl + Q to instantly pull up the search overlay, type a few letters of the file you need, and press Enter. It searches across your entire Vault in milliseconds using deep SQLite FTS5 indexing.

Alt + V : Toggle Vault Sidebar
Need more screen space? Press Alt + V to collapse or expand the left-hand folder view. Perfect for deep focus when reading dense PDFs or writing long essays.

Ctrl + T : New Web Panel
Researching online? Don't break your context by switching to a separate browser. Press Ctrl + T to open a lightweight web panel directly alongside your notes.

Ctrl + Shift + T : Reopen Closed Tab
Accidentally closed a crucial reading? Ctrl + Shift + T brings it right back exactly where you left off.
